HISTORICAL

RED FORT




1)The Red Fort Complex was built as the palace fort of Shahjahanabad – the new capital of Shah Jahan.
2)Named for its massive enclosing walls of red sandstone, adjacent to an older fort, Salimgarh, built by Islam Shah Suri in 1546.
3)The private apartments consist of a row of pavilions connected by a continuous water channel-Nahr-i-Behisht (Stream of Paradise).
4)The Red Fort is considered to represent the zenith of Mughal creativity.
5)The planning of the palace is based on Islamic prototypes, but each pavilion reveals architectural elements typical of Mughal building.
6)The Red Fort’s innovative planning and architectural style, including the garden design, strongly influenced later buildings and gardens.
7)This palace fortress is known as the Red Fort because of the red sandstone fabric of its rampart walls.
